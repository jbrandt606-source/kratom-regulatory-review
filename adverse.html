Kratom Adverse Event Reporting

This page summarizes publicly available adverse event reports, federal safety communications, and clinical surveillance data related to kratom (Mitragyna speciosa) exposure.

Adverse event reporting systems collect voluntary submissions from clinicians, consumers, and manufacturers. These reports describe reported associations and do not independently establish causation.

FDA CAERS Adverse Event Reports
CFSAN Adverse Event Reporting System (CAERS)

The FDA’s CAERS database collects reports involving foods and dietary supplements. Reports referencing kratom products have included serious medical outcomes such as hospitalization, liver injury, seizures, dependence, and deaths.

Documented Reported Outcomes
  • Hospitalizations following kratom ingestion
  • Reported deaths in submitted adverse event cases
  • Liver injury and elevated liver enzymes
  • Seizures and neurologic events
  • Substance use disorder and withdrawal symptoms

Clinical Case Reports and Literature Summaries
Reported Clinical Effects
  • Hepatic injury (liver inflammation, cholestasis)
  • Neurologic complications (seizure, altered mental status)
  • Psychiatric disturbance (psychosis, paranoia)
  • Cardiovascular effects (tachycardia, arrhythmias)
  • Gastrointestinal symptoms (nausea, vomiting, constipation)
Limitations of Current Reporting

Many reports involve co-ingestion of other substances, potential contamination, or incomplete toxicology data. Adverse event reports do not provide incidence rates and are not designed to establish direct causality.

Public Health Context

The U.S. Food and Drug Administration has issued multiple public safety communications regarding kratom products, citing concerns about contamination, toxicity, and substance use disorder risk. There are currently no FDA-approved kratom products in U.S. commerce.
