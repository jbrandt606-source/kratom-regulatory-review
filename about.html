About This Site

Kratom Regulatory Review is an informational public health resource that compiles publicly available federal surveillance data and peer-reviewed scientific literature related to kratom (Mitragyna speciosa) and its alkaloids.

Purpose

The site consolidates data from national mortality surveillance systems, FDA adverse event reporting systems (FAERS and CAERS), the National Poison Data System (NPDS), and published clinical case reports. These materials are organized to facilitate structured review in legislative and regulatory contexts.

Content is limited to publicly accessible sources, including federal databases, official reports, and peer-reviewed literature.

Scope and Limitations

Surveillance systems and adverse event reports describe reported exposures and outcomes. These data do not independently establish incidence rates or causation and may include cases involving multiple substances.

This site does not provide medical advice and does not advocate specific regulatory actions. It serves as an educational reference compiling publicly available information.

Sources

Data and literature presented on this site are derived from:

  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)
  • U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA)
  • National Poison Data System (NPDS)
  • Peer-reviewed publications indexed in PubMed
