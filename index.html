Overview

Kratom (Mitragyna speciosa) is a plant-derived substance containing multiple alkaloids, including mitragynine and 7-hydroxymitragynine. This review consolidates publicly available federal surveillance reporting, adverse event databases, poison control exposure data, regulatory communications, and peer-reviewed scientific literature.

The purpose of this site is to provide a centralized reference resource for legislative, regulatory, and research staff evaluating public health considerations associated with kratom.

Mortality Surveillance

Federal and state surveillance systems have reported cases in which kratom alkaloids were identified in postmortem toxicology. This section summarizes available CDC reporting, including instances where kratom was listed among substances detected.

Reporting methodologies, co-occurring substances, and classification practices are noted where available.

Adverse Event Reporting

The U.S. Food and Drug Administration maintains adverse event reporting systems that include submissions referencing kratom-containing products. This section compiles publicly accessible data from FDA databases, including reported clinical outcomes and recall communications.

Adverse event submissions do not independently establish causation but provide surveillance indicators for further review.

Poison Control Exposure Data

National Poison Data System (NPDS) reporting includes calls referencing kratom exposures. This section summarizes exposure trends, reported clinical effects, and severity classifications.

Data are presented as surveillance summaries and do not reflect independent medical adjudication.

Peer-Reviewed Scientific Literature

Published literature describes pharmacologic properties of mitragynine and 7-hydroxymitragynine, including receptor binding profiles, dose-response characteristics, dependence potential, and withdrawal syndromes.

This section compiles peer-reviewed toxicology, clinical case reports, and experimental studies relevant to regulatory evaluation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is kratom?

Kratom refers to the plant Mitragyna speciosa, which contains multiple alkaloids, including mitragynine and 7-hydroxymitragynine. These compounds have been studied for pharmacologic activity.

What types of public health data are available?

Publicly available data include CDC mortality surveillance, FDA adverse event reporting systems, poison control exposure summaries, and peer-reviewed toxicology literature.

Does this site make policy recommendations?

No. This site compiles publicly available information for educational and legislative reference. It does not advocate specific regulatory actions.

Do adverse event reports establish causation?

Adverse event submissions provide surveillance signals but do not independently establish causation without further scientific or clinical evaluation.
