Kratom Public Health Surveillance Review

Compilation of publicly available federal surveillance data, adverse event reporting systems, poison control exposure summaries, regulatory communications, and peer-reviewed literature related to Mitragyna speciosa and its alkaloids.

Overview

Kratom (Mitragyna speciosa) is a plant-derived substance containing multiple alkaloids, including mitragynine and 7-hydroxymitragynine. This site consolidates publicly accessible federal and scientific sources relevant to legislative and regulatory evaluation.

Sources include mortality surveillance systems, adverse event databases, poison control exposure reporting, regulatory safety communications, and peer-reviewed pharmacologic and toxicologic research.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is kratom?

Kratom refers to the plant Mitragyna speciosa, which contains alkaloids including mitragynine and 7-hydroxymitragynine that have documented pharmacologic activity.

Does this site make policy recommendations?

No. This site compiles publicly available federal and scientific information for educational and legislative reference. It does not advocate specific regulatory actions.

Do adverse event reports establish causation?

Adverse event submissions provide surveillance indicators but do not independently establish causation without further scientific evaluation.
